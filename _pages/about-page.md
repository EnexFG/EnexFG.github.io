I am an applied econometrician working across industry, academia, and public policy. I currently serve as Chief Economist at Andersen Ecuador and teach at ESPOL and Ecotec.

I advise private and public institutions on data-informed decision-making and contribute to academia through applied research.

My work centers on macroeconometrics and forecasting, Bayesian methods, and natural language processing, combining statistical rigor with computational tools for measurement and prediction.

I like building analytical frameworks that are technically serious but operationally useful. That means combining model design, data work, interpretation, and communication so that analysis can actually travel into decisions.

When I'm not doing statistics, I'm, on average, either drinking wine or scuba diving.
